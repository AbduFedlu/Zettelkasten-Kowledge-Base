# Note 

--- 

- The first exercises should be simple ones. 
- Simple Exercises are easier to adapt without extra effort 
- If the first exercises are complex you may cut down from your future gains. 

--- 

-  Train As Much As You Need, Not As Much As You Can

   > If you don’t make progress and it’s too easy… fantastic, just do more volume and now you know more about your body’s needs. But in my experience as a coach, it’s just as likely (if not more likely) that you could progress just as well, if not faster, **with a lower volume**.

6 Steps to make a Training program



1.  Adherence : Plan your workout frequency with **adherence in mind**
    
- The main takeaway of Level 1 is selecting how many days per week you train. Choose a **realistic number** of training days that would not stress your life or schedule.
  
 2.  Volume -> Intensity -> Frequency

  - Volume (sets) : **4 sets per muscle group per week to see results in 2-3 months time.**
     - When determinig volume optimal might mean the lowest volume that still produces good returns with minimal time.
  - Frequency (days/week) : The higher your determined volume -> the more it should be spread over the week. so you can: 
     -  Prevent longer sessions
	 -  Quality controll
  - Intensity (kg and reps) : The general approach should avoid passiveness. With heavy mind on you RIR (reps in reserve). for it is important in exerting enough force on your muscles and actually producing change. 

> Note on Intensity :
> - Do not use "Reps in Reserve" (RIR). Do not stop just because you hit a target number (e.g., 10 reps) if you could have done 5 or 10 more. because you are doing so few sets
> - You must train with low RIR (0-2) . This means leaving only 0-2 reps "in the tank" – or going all the way to failure (0 RIR) on **your final set**. because you are doing so few sets
> > -  Make sure you define your RIR values for the respective set. 


3.  Progression : deterimining and adjusting future goals of your exercise. the rates of progress for you the novice is put as:

- Able to add load and/or reps each time a lift is repeated in the same week or from week to week. This phase can last for years but may be as short as 6 months with consistent, progressive training, sufficient recovery, and effort.

 4. Excersice selection : The excersise selection should be automated with AI (preferably deepseek with websearch turned off). if your still aiming for a full body workout.

 5. Rest periods : good rest periods include:

- Drop Sets: Execute a set to failure, reduce weight by 20–30%, and immediately continue to failure. This increases metabolic stress and recruits high-threshold motor units.
- Rest-Pause: Perform a set to failure, rest for 10–15 seconds, then perform additional repetitions with the same weight. This bypasses the phosphagen system's full recovery to extend the effective reps near muscular failure.
